# 📊 Customer Retention & Churn Analysis

## 🔍 Project Overview

This project focuses on analyzing customer churn behavior in a subscription-based business. The goal is to identify patterns, understand why customers leave, and suggest strategies to improve customer retention.

---

## 🎯 Objectives

* Analyze customer churn rate and retention trends
* Identify high-risk customer segments
* Understand the impact of tenure, contract type, and pricing
* Provide actionable business recommendations

---

## 📂 Dataset Description

The dataset includes the following key columns:

* **Customer ID** – Unique identifier
* **Gender** – Customer gender
* **Tenure (Months)** – Duration of customer relationship
* **Monthly Charges** – Amount charged per month
* **Contract Type** – Month-to-month, One year, Two year
* **Churn** – Yes (1) / No (0)

---

## 🧹 Data Cleaning

* Removed missing and null values
* Converted churn values (Yes = 1, No = 0)
* Ensured correct data types (numeric, categorical)
* Checked and removed duplicate records

## 📈 Analysis Performed

### 🔹 Churn by Contract Type

* Month-to-month customers have the highest churn
* Long-term contracts show lower churn

### 🔹 Churn by Tenure

* New customers (0–6 months) have the highest churn
* Longer tenure customers are more stable

### 🔹 Churn by Monthly Charges

* Higher monthly charges are associated with increased churn

### 🔹 Customer Distribution

* Majority of customers are on month-to-month contracts

---

## 🔍 Key Insights

* High churn is observed in short-term contracts
* Early-stage customers are more likely to leave
* Pricing plays a significant role in customer retention
* Long-term engagement reduces churn probability

---

## 💡 Business Recommendations

* Encourage long-term subscriptions through discounts
* Improve onboarding experience for new users
* Offer flexible pricing plans
* Focus retention efforts on high-risk segments
